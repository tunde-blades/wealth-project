Estate Planning Documents You Need

A last will and testament is a legal document that names the executor and communicates a person’s final wishes pertaining to assets and beneficiaries.

A revocable trust is often used in estate planning to accomplish the settlor’s testamentary intent, while avoiding the probate process. The pour-over will is a truncated will that transfers the estate into the trust.

In case something happens and your children need a guardian, these are non-binding instructions to the judge on who you would like to care for your children.

An advance health care directive allows you to indicate who should make decisions and what your wishes are about your health care. Legally, the only binding part may be picking the agent.

A durable power of attorney allows you to indicate who should manage your financial affairs if something were to happen to you.
